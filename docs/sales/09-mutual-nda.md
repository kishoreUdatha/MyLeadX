## 1. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information, in any form, disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party") in connection with the Purpose, whether before or after the Effective Date, including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, financial information, pricing, and forecasts
- Customer, prospect, employee, and supplier data
- Technical information, source code, algorithms, models, prompts, and architecture
- Product roadmaps, unreleased features, designs, and specifications
- Marketing plans, sales materials, and competitive analyses
- Any information marked "Confidential" or that, by its nature, a reasonable person would understand to be confidential

Confidential Information may be disclosed **orally, in writing, electronically, visually, or in any other form**.

---

## 2. Exclusions

Confidential Information does **not** include information that:

| # | Exclusion |
|---|-----------|
| 1 | Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party |
| 2 | Was lawfully in the Receiving Party's possession before disclosure, without any confidentiality obligation |
| 3 | Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information |
| 4 | Is rightfully obtained from a third party without restriction on disclosure |

The burden of proving an exclusion rests with the Receiving Party.

## 3. Obligations of the Receiving Party

The Receiving Party shall:

### 3.1 Permitted Use

Use the Confidential Information **solely** for the Purpose, and for no other reason.

### 3.2 Standard of Care

Protect the Confidential Information using at least the same degree of care it uses for its own confidential information of a similar nature, and in any event no less than **reasonable care**.

### 3.3 Limited Disclosure

Disclose the Confidential Information only to its employees, directors, officers, contractors, and professional advisors (collectively "Representatives") who:

- Have a legitimate need to know for the Purpose
- Have been informed of the confidential nature of the information
- Are bound by written obligations of confidentiality at least as protective as those set out in this Agreement

### 3.4 Responsibility for Representatives

Each Party remains responsible and liable for any breach of this Agreement by its Representatives.

### 3.5 No Reverse Engineering

Not reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or attempt to derive the source code, algorithms, or underlying ideas from any software, model, or prototype disclosed.

### 3.6 No IP Rights Granted

Nothing in this Agreement grants any licence or other right to any patent, trademark, copyright, trade secret, or other intellectual property, except the limited right to use the Confidential Information for the Purpose.

## 4. Required Disclosures

If the Receiving Party is required by law, regulation, or a valid court or governmental order to disclose any Confidential Information, it shall:

1. Provide **prompt written notice** to the Disclosing Party (where legally permitted) before disclosure
2. Cooperate reasonably with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party's expense, to seek a protective order or other remedy
3. Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required
4. Use reasonable efforts to obtain assurance that the information will be treated confidentially

---

## 5. Return or Destruction

Upon the earlier of (a) written request by the Disclosing Party, or (b) termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall, within thirty (30) days:

- Return all tangible Confidential Information and all copies thereof, **or**
- At the Disclosing Party's option, destroy all such Confidential Information and, upon request, certify the destruction in writing signed by an authorized officer

The Receiving Party may retain one (1) archival copy solely for legal or compliance purposes, subject to continuing confidentiality obligations. Confidential Information retained in routine backup systems may be retained until ordinary deletion occurs, provided it remains subject to this Agreement.

---

## 6. Term

### 6.1 Term of Agreement

This Agreement begins on the Effective Date and continues for **two (2) years**, unless terminated earlier by either Party on thirty (30) days written notice.

### 6.2 Survival of Confidentiality

The confidentiality obligations under this Agreement shall survive for **three (3) years** from the date of termination or expiration, except that:

- Obligations with respect to **trade secrets** continue for as long as such information qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law
- Obligations with respect to **personal data** continue in accordance with applicable data protection law
